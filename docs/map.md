# Interactive Map

## 🗺️ Explore Persian Poetry Through Geography and Time

Our interactive map is the centerpiece of the Persian Poetry Map project, offering a unique way to explore the geographical spread and historical development of Persian poetry.

## 🎮 How to Use the Map

### Navigation Controls

- **Pan & Zoom** - Click and drag to move around, scroll or use +/- buttons to zoom
- **Time Slider** - Located at the bottom, slide through 6 historical eras
- **City Markers** - Click on bold city markers to reveal detailed information
- **Layer Toggle** - Show/hide different types of information

### Time Periods Available

Navigate through these significant eras using the time slider:

| Era       | Time Period    | Key Poets          |
| --------- | -------------- | ------------------ |
| Samanid   | 875 – 1000 CE  | Rudaki, Daqiqi     |
| Ghaznavid | 1000 – 1150 CE | Ferdowsi, Unsuri   |
| Seljuk    | 1150 – 1300 CE | Nizami, Khaqani    |
| Ilkhanate | 1260 – 1370 CE | Saadi, Rumi        |
| Timurid   | 1370 – 1500 CE | Hafez, Jami        |
| Safavid   | 1500 – 1736 CE | Baba Tahir, Vahshi |

## 🏙️ City Markers

Cities are marked with different styles:

- **Bold markers** - Major cultural centers with extensive poet activity
- Regular markers - Cities with notable but less prominent literary history

Click on any city to discover:

- Historical significance
- Associated poets
- Notable works created there
- Architectural landmarks related to poetry

## 🎨 Visual Features

- **Historical Borders** - Dynamic rendering of changing political boundaries
- **Color Coding** - Different colors represent different types of information
- **Responsive Design** - Works seamlessly on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices

## 💡 Tips for Best Experience

1. Start from the earliest era and progress chronologically
2. Pay attention to cities that appear across multiple eras
3. Notice how cultural centers shifted over time
4. Use zoom to see detailed information in dense regions
